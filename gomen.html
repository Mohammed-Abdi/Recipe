Gomen: Ethiopia's Collard Greens Stew
Gomen is a delicious Ethiopian dish made with collard greens, garlic, and a variety of aromatic spices. This flavorful and nutritious vegetarian stew is often served with injera, making it a perfect accompaniment to any meal.
Ingredient Quantity
Collard greens (chopped) 1 bunch
Onions (finely chopped) 2 medium
Garlic (minced) 4 cloves
Ginger (grated) 1 teaspoon
Green chili peppers (optional) 2
Turmeric powder 1 teaspoon
Niter Kibbeh (spiced butter) or oil 2 tablespoons
Water 1 cup
Salt To taste

Instructions

1. Prepare the Collard Greens

Wash the collard greens thoroughly and chop them into small pieces. Set aside.

2. Cook the Onions

In a large pot, sauté the onions over medium heat with Niter Kibbeh or oil until soft and golden, about 5–7 minutes.

3. Add Aromatics

Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chili peppers (if using). Cook for 2–3 minutes until fragrant.

4. Add Turmeric

Stir in the turmeric powder and cook for another minute, allowing the spices to blend together.

5. Add Collard Greens

Add the chopped collard greens to the pot. Stir to combine with the spices and aromatics.

6. Add Water and Simmer

Pour in the water and bring to a simmer. Cover and cook for 15–20 minutes until the collard greens are tender and well-cooked.

7. Season and Serve

Add salt to taste and serve the Gomen hot with injera or rice.
