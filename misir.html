Misir Wat: Ethiopia's Spiced Lentil Stew
Misir Wat is a classic Ethiopian lentil stew made with red lentils, rich spices, and berbere seasoning. It's a comforting, nutritious dish that pairs perfectly with injera or rice. This vegan-friendly recipe is full of bold flavors and a hearty texture.
Ingredient Quantity
Red lentils 1 cup
Onions (finely chopped) 2 medium
Garlic (minced) 3 cloves
Ginger (grated) 1 teaspoon
Berbere spice blend 2 tablespoons
Tomato paste 2 tablespoons
Vegetable stock or water 3 cups
Niter Kibbeh (spiced butter) or oil 2 tablespoons
Salt To taste

Instructions

1. Rinse the Lentils

Wash the red lentils thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. Set aside.

2. Cook the Onions

In a large pot, sauté the onions over medium heat without oil until soft and golden. Stir frequently to prevent sticking.

3. Add Aromatics

Stir in the minced garlic, grated ginger, and Niter Kibbeh or oil. Cook for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.

4. Add Berbere Spice

Add the berbere spice blend and cook for another minute, stirring constantly to release its aroma.

5. Mix in Tomato Paste

Add the tomato paste and mix well. Cook for 2 minutes, ensuring the paste is well incorporated.

6. Add Lentils and Stock

Add the rinsed lentils and vegetable stock or water. Stir to combine, then bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let simmer for 20–25 minutes until the lentils are tender.

7. Season and Serve

Add salt to taste. Serve the Misir Wat hot with injera, rice, or bread.
