Doro Wat: Ethiopia's Signature Chicken Stew
Doro Wat is a rich and flavorful Ethiopian chicken stew, celebrated for its deep, spicy flavors and vibrant red color. Made with tender chicken, spiced clarified butter, and berbere spice blend, it's traditionally served with injera, a sourdough flatbread. Perfect for gatherings or a hearty meal.
Ingredient Quantity
Chicken (cut into pieces) 1 kg
Onions (finely chopped) 4 large
Garlic (minced) 4 cloves
Ginger (grated) 1 tablespoon
Berbere spice blend 3 tablespoons
Niter Kibbeh (spiced butter) 4 tablespoons
Tomato paste 2 tablespoons
Chicken stock or water 1 cup
Lemon juice 2 tablespoons
Hard-boiled eggs 4
Salt To taste

Instructions

1. Prepare the Chicken

Wash the chicken pieces thoroughly and marinate with lemon juice and a pinch of salt. Set aside.

2. Cook the Onions

In a large pot, cook the onions over medium heat without oil until soft and slightly caramelized. Stir constantly to avoid burning.

3. Add Aromatics

Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and Niter Kibbeh. Stir well for about 2 minutes.

4. Incorporate Berbere Spice

Add the berbere spice blend and cook for another 2 minutes, stirring continuously to release its aroma.

5. Mix Tomato Paste and Stock

Stir in the tomato paste and chicken stock, ensuring everything is well combined. Let it simmer for 5 minutes.

6. Add Chicken

Place the marinated chicken pieces into the pot, coating them thoroughly with the sauce. Cover and simmer on low heat for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally.

7. Add Eggs

Peel the hard-boiled eggs and make small slits on their sides. Add them to the stew during the last 10 minutes of cooking.

8. Season and Serve

Adjust salt to taste and serve the Doro Wat hot with injera or rice.
