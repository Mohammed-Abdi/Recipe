Fetira: Ethiopian Layered Flatbread
Fetira is a traditional Ethiopian layered flatbread, often enjoyed for breakfast. It's flaky and crispy, typically served with honey, eggs, or yogurt for a delicious and filling meal.
Ingredient Quantity
All-purpose flour 2 cups
Warm water 3/4 cup
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Vegetable oil (or Niter Kibbeh) 1/4 cup
Butter (melted) 1/4 cup
Egg (optional) 1, for brushing

Instructions

1. Prepare the Dough

In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt. Gradually add the warm water, stirring to form a dough. Knead until smooth, about 5-7 minutes. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.

2. Roll Out the Dough

Divide the dough into small portions. Roll each portion into a thin, round sheet, as thin as possible without tearing.

3. Layer and Fold

Brush each rolled dough with melted butter or oil. Stack the sheets on top of each other, folding the edges in to form a rectangular or square shape. You can repeat this process for multiple layers to make the flatbread crispy and flaky.

4. Cook the Fetira

Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Place the layered dough in the pan and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy.

5. Optional: Brush with Egg

If you like, brush the cooked Fetira with a beaten egg for a glossy finish.

6. Serve

Serve warm with honey, eggs, or yogurt, and enjoy!
