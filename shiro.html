Shiro Wat: Ethiopia's Chickpea Stew
Shiro Wat is a flavorful Ethiopian stew made with chickpea flour and aromatic spices. This vegetarian dish is simple to prepare, hearty, and pairs perfectly with injera or bread. A staple in Ethiopian households, it's loved for its creamy texture and delicious taste.
Ingredient Quantity
Shiro (chickpea flour) 1 cup
Onions (finely chopped) 2 medium
Garlic (minced) 3 cloves
Ginger (grated) 1 teaspoon
Berbere spice blend 1 tablespoon
Niter Kibbeh (spiced butter) or oil 2 tablespoons
Water 3 cups
Salt To taste

Instructions

1. Prepare the Base

In a pot, sauté the onions over medium heat without oil until soft and golden. Stir frequently to avoid burning.

2. Add Aromatics

Stir in the minced garlic, grated ginger, and Niter Kibbeh or oil. Cook for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.

3. Add Berbere Spice

Add the berbere spice blend and cook for another minute, stirring constantly to release its aroma.

4. Add Water

Gradually add the water while stirring to prevent lumps. Bring to a gentle boil.

5. Add Shiro Flour

Slowly whisk in the chickpea flour, ensuring no clumps form. Lower the heat and let it simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally.

6. Season and Serve

Add salt to taste. Serve the Shiro Wat hot with injera or bread.
